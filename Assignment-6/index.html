SAHARA

The Sahara Desert is the largest hot desert in the world, stretching across much of North Africa. Covering an area of about 9.2 million square kilometers (3.6 million square miles), it is nearly the size of the United States or China. The Sahara spans 11 countries, including Algeria, Chad, Egypt, Libya, Mali, Mauritania, Morocco, Niger, Sudan, Tunisia, and Western Sahara. The Sahara is known for its extreme climate, with scorching daytime temperatures that can exceed 50°C (122°F) in summer, and much cooler nights, sometimes dropping near freezing in winter. Rainfall is rare and unpredictable, averaging less than 25 mm (1 inch) annually in most areas. The desert landscape varies greatly, featuring vast sand dunes (ergs), rocky plateaus (hamadas), gravel plains (regs), mountains, and occasional oases.
